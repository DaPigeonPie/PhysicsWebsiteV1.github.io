Challenge Question 1

A square coil of copper wire, with 12 loops and diameter 0.25 meters, is rotating inside a solenoid at an angular velocity of 60 revolutions per minute. The strength of the magnetic field is a uniform 2 Tesla, and the square coil is rotating with its axis of rotation perpendicular to the magnetic field lines such that the EMF oscillates between peak power and zero power. Initially, the normal of the wire loop area is parallel to the magnetic field lines. The linear resistivity of the copper coil is 18 ohms per meter of wire at SATP.

(a) Find the function of EMF with respect to time.

(b) Find the function of power with respect to time.
